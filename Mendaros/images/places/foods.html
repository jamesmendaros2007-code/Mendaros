Popular Foods

Humba

Pagadian City

Humba: A Filipino Braised Pork Belly Dish Humba is a popular Filipino dish from the Visayan region, specifically from the island of Cebu. It's a braised pork belly dish that's sweet, savory, and tender. The main ingredients are: - Pork belly (the star of the show!) - Soy sauce - Vinegar - Brown sugar or muscovado sugar - Garlic - Bay leaves - Star anise (optional) The pork belly is slow-cooked in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, and sugar until it's tender and flavorful. The result is a melt-in-your-mouth texture with a balance of sweet, salty, and tangy flavors. Humba is often served with steamed rice and is a favorite in Filipino cuisine, especially during special occasions.

Sinigang

Pagadian City

Sinigang: A Sour and Savory Filipino Soup Sinigang is a classic Filipino soup dish known for its sour and savory broth. It's a staple in Filipino cuisine and is often served with steamed rice. The broth is made with: - Tamarind (sampalok) or other souring agents like calamansi or green mango - Various proteins like pork, shrimp, fish, or chicken - Vegetables like tomatoes, onions, and leafy greens (kangkong or spinach) - Seasonings like fish sauce or patis The sourness of the broth is the star of the show, balanced by the savory flavors of the protein and vegetables. Sinigang is comforting and refreshing, perfect for any time of day.

Chicken curry

Pagadian City

Chicken Curry: A Flavorful and Aromatic Dish Chicken curry is a popular dish that's savory, slightly spicy, and full of flavor. It's made with chicken cooked in a rich and creamy curry sauce with a blend of spices and herbs. Typical ingredients include: - Chicken (breast or thighs) - Curry powder or paste - Coconut milk or cream - Onions, garlic, and ginger - Spices like cumin, coriander, and turmeric - Vegetables like potatoes and carrots (optional) The curry sauce is where the magic happens - it's a balance of spices, aromatics, and creaminess that makes the chicken tender and delicious. Chicken curry is often served with steamed rice or naan bread.
